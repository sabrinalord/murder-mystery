The Characters

The General

A rich, retired army general - he likes a joke as long as it's not on him. He's pompous and a show off and has a ruthless eye for making money.

Lady Lord

Arrogant, spoilt and charming when she wants to be. Lady Lord is the General's much younger second wife.

The Game Hunter

Younger brother to the General and more brute than brains. Almost as pompous and not nearly as successful - he's been in his brother's shadow all his life.

The Soldier

The eldest son of the general, a writer and a sensitive soul. He's just back from fighting in the war and he's got the scars to prove it.

The Activist

A glamourous champion of women's rights. People say she bears a striking resemblence to Louise Brooks. She hates it when she doesn't get her own way and thinks that revenge is best served in style.

The Landlady

A real class act - she owns the Bodega club with her husband and partner in crime. She's a no-nonsense ruffian who always stands up for her friends.

The Landlord

Owner of the Bodega club. An impeccable criminal and hardened gangster, he always gets what he wants one way or another.

The Dancer

A whirlwind of feathers and sequins, she's the star act of the Bodega club and could dazzle anyone with her smile. Don't get too enamoured, she's married to The Poker Player.

The Poker Player

A Slick Rick and the best card player at the Bodega. He's married to the Dancer and even she can't tell when he's bluffing. He's an incessant gambler with a short fuse. Watch out - he'll bleed you dry if you're not careful.

The Accountant

Clever and seemingly sensible, but can The Accountant be trusted? He's been looking after the General's finances for years. You could say he is heavily invested in the welfare of the estate.

The Chef

Unpredictable and rebellious, the Chef has been waiting for a day off for months. If only the General would give him the time of day to notice.

The Priest

Priest of the local parish church, a Catholic man of faith and a sympathetic ear to the towns darkest confessions. Some would say the Priest's been looking a little jumpy lately, especially when the General is near by...

The Clairvoyant

She's a well known mystic and after several correct predictions, the Clairvoyant's become somewhat of a celebrity in town. She's a close friend of Lady Lord and is as famous for her dramatics as she is for her psychic abilities.

The Doctor

Friend and confidant of the General's late, former wife. The doctor know's the ins and outs of each family member... but where does her loyalties lie?

The Maid

The maid has to put up with the General's violent temper. She's seen and heard everything, but does she know too much?

The Ex-Detective

Sacked from Scotland Yard due to a string of wrong convictions, the Ex-Detective has had to dust herself off from a rather public sacking. She loves a good mystery, but does she know how to solve one?

The Journalist

Don't be fooled by her charm - the Journalist is devious and devilishly wicked. She's got eyes and ears everywhere so think twice before double crossing her.

HOW TO PLAY

  • The theme is Halloween, 1920s - try and look identifiable to your character.
  • When you arrive you'll get an envelope with information and a secret task.
  • Act as much or as little as you like but try to stay in character.
  • You don't have to read any lines.

Getting here

  • Get the train from London Victoria to Harrietsham (1.20min)
  • Get to station for 4pm. (Before it gets dark - there are no street lights)
  • At Harrietsham station, go to platform 2 and follow the dirt footpath, its a 20 minute walk. The footpath is a shortcut and doesn't show on google maps.
  • The murder mystery will be over by 10pm. You can get the train home or you can stay for party time afterwards. Everyone is welcome to stay over.
